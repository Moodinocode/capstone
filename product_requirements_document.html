Executive Summary** This document outlines the requirements for **SmartPlate AI**, a mobile application designed to simplify healthy eating, reduce food waste, and streamline grocery shopping through AI-powered personalized meal planning. The application will leverage user dietary preferences, existing ingredients, and health goals to generate tailored meal plans and automatically create optimized grocery lists, aiming to make nutritious eating accessible and efficient for everyone. ### **2. Introduction** #### **2.1. Background / Problem Statement** Many individuals and families struggle with consistent meal planning due to time constraints, lack of inspiration, dietary restrictions, and budgeting challenges. This often leads to unhealthy eating habits, increased food waste from unused ingredients, and inefficient grocery shopping trips. Existing solutions are often generic, complex, or lack true personalization. #### **2.2. Vision / Goal** Our vision is to become the leading personalized meal planning platform that empowers users to eat healthier, save time, reduce food waste, and simplify their lives by intelligently connecting dietary needs, recipes, and grocery procurement. #### **2.3. Scope (Initial Release - MVP)** **In-Scope:** * User profile creation and management (dietary preferences, allergies, health goals). * AI-powered personalized meal plan generation (daily/weekly). * Searchable recipe database with filters. * Ability to swap/edit suggested meals within a plan. * Automated grocery list generation from the meal plan, categorized by aisle. * Manual editing of grocery lists. * Ingredient inventory feature (input what you have to influence suggestions). * Basic user authentication (email/password, social login). **Out-of-Scope (for MVP):** * Direct grocery delivery integration. * Barcode scanning for inventory management. * Advanced nutrition tracking (macronutrients, micronutrients). * Community features (sharing plans, recipes). * Smart appliance integration. * Multi-user profiles for families. #### **2.4. Target Audience** * **Health-Conscious Individuals:** Seeking personalized, easy-to-follow healthy eating plans. * **Busy Professionals/Families:** Who lack time for meal planning and grocery list creation. * **Individuals with Dietary Restrictions:** (e.g., vegan, gluten-free, keto, low-sodium) needing tailored meal suggestions. * **Budget-Conscious Shoppers:** Aiming to reduce food waste and optimize grocery spending. ### **3. Business Objectives & Key Results (OKRs)** * **Objective 1:** Establish SmartPlate AI as the go-to app for personalized meal planning. * **KR 1.1:** Achieve 10,000 active weekly users within 3 months post-launch. * **KR 1.2:** Maintain a 4-week user retention rate of over 30%. * **KR 1.3:** Achieve an average App Store/Google Play rating of 4.5 stars. * **Objective 2:** Drive significant user engagement and value. * **KR 2.1:** Users create an average of 2 meal plans per week. * **KR 2.2:** Users generate an average of 1.5 grocery lists per week. * **KR 2.3:** Over 70% of users report saving time on meal planning and grocery shopping (via in-app survey). * **Objective 3:** Validate potential for future monetization. * **KR 3.1:** Identify top 3 most requested premium features through user feedback. (This objective focuses on *learning* for future monetization, not direct revenue yet in MVP). ### **4. User Stories / Key Features** * **User Profile & Preferences:** * As a new user, I want to create a profile with my dietary preferences (e.g., vegetarian, gluten-free), allergies (e.g., nuts, dairy), and health goals (e.g., weight loss, muscle gain) so that I receive relevant meal suggestions. * As a user, I want to update my profile at any time so that my meal plans reflect my current needs. * **Meal Plan Generation:** * As a user, I want to receive a personalized weekly meal plan based on my profile, current ingredients, and past preferences so that I don't have to plan from scratch. * As a user, I want to be able to swap out suggested meals for alternatives so that I have flexibility in my plan. * As a user, I want to view detailed recipe instructions, ingredients, and estimated cooking time for each meal. * As a user, I want to save favorite recipes for easy access later. * **Grocery List Generation:** * As a user, I want to automatically generate a grocery list from my weekly meal plan so that I don't forget any ingredients. * As a user, I want the grocery list to be categorized (e.g., produce, dairy, pantry) so that shopping is more efficient. * As a user, I want to manually add or remove items from my grocery list. * As a user, I want to input ingredients I already have at home so that they are excluded from the grocery list. * **Core Experience:** * As a user, I want to easily navigate the app and understand its features so that I can use it effectively. * As a user, I want reliable performance and fast loading times so that my experience is smooth. ### **5. Functional Requirements** 1. **Authentication Module:** * User registration (email/password). * Login/Logout. * Password reset. * Social login integration (Google, Apple). 2. **User Profile Management:** * Capture and store dietary preferences (vegetarian, vegan, keto, paleo, etc.). * Capture and store allergy information. * Capture and store health goals (weight loss, maintenance, muscle gain). * Ability to update profile details. 3. **Recipe Database & Search:** * Extensive, curated recipe database (initial 500-1000 recipes). * Ability to filter recipes by cuisine, meal type, dietary tags, cooking time, ingredients. * Display detailed recipe information (ingredients, instructions, nutrition estimates, prep/cook time, serving size). * Rating/feedback system for recipes. 4. **AI Meal Plan Generator:** * Algorithm to generate daily/weekly meal plans based on user profile, ingredient inventory, and past interactions. * Ensures variety and nutritional balance. * Provides options for breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks. 5. **Meal Plan Editor:** * Drag-and-drop interface for swapping meals within a plan. * Option to manually add a recipe to a specific day/meal slot. * Ability to mark meals as "cooked." 6. **Grocery List Module:** * Automatic consolidation of ingredients from the weekly meal plan. * Categorization of ingredients (e.g., produce, dairy, meats, pantry, frozen). * User-editable list (add, remove, mark as purchased). * Integration with user's ingredient inventory to exclude owned items. 7. **Ingredient Inventory:** * Manual input of ingredients currently on hand. * Option to specify quantity and expiry (optional for MVP). 8. **Push Notifications (Basic):** * Reminders for meal prep. * Notification when a new weekly plan is ready. ### **6. Non-Functional Requirements** * **Performance:** * Meal plan generation within 5 seconds. * Recipe search results loaded within 2 seconds. * App launch time under 3 seconds. * **Scalability:** * Architecture must support growth to 100,000+ users without significant performance degradation. * Database capable of handling increasing recipe and user data. * **Security:** * User data (profile, preferences) encrypted at rest and in transit. * Secure authentication protocols. * Regular security audits. * **Usability & UX:** * Intuitive, clean, and consistent user interface (UI) and user experience (UX). * Mobile-first design (iOS & Android native apps). * Accessibility considerations (e.g., legible fonts, color contrast). * **Reliability:** * 99.9% uptime for core services. * Robust error handling and logging. * **Maintainability:** * Well-documented codebase. * Modular architecture for future feature expansion. ### **7. Technical Considerations (High-Level)** * **Backend:** Cloud-based (AWS, GCP, Azure), API-driven (e.g., Node.js, Python/Django, Ruby on Rails). * **Database:** PostgreSQL for relational data, potentially a NoSQL for flexible recipe data (e.g., MongoDB). * **AI/ML:** Recommendation engine using collaborative filtering and content-based approaches. May involve leveraging external ML services or building in-house. * **Frontend:** Native mobile apps (Swift/Kotlin) or cross-platform (React Native/Flutter) based on team expertise and budget. * **Third-Party Integrations:** Payment gateway (for future premium), social login APIs, potentially a curated recipe API. ### **8. Dependencies & Risks** * **Dependencies:** * Access to a high-quality, diverse, and well-structured recipe database. * Successful training and continuous improvement of the AI recommendation model. * Talented product, design, and engineering team. * **Risks:** * **Data Privacy Concerns:** Users may be hesitant to share detailed dietary and health data. (Mitigation: Transparent privacy policy, robust security). * **AI Bias/Quality:** Recommendations might be repetitive, unappetizing, or not truly personalized. (Mitigation: Extensive testing, user feedback loops, continuous model refinement). * **User Adoption:** Competition from existing meal kit services, general recipe apps, or free content. (Mitigation: Strong value proposition, superior UX, targeted marketing). * **Recipe Data Licensing:** Cost or limitations in sourcing a comprehensive recipe database. (Mitigation: Explore partnerships, manual curation). ### **9. Success Metrics & Analytics** * **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):** * Weekly Active Users (WAU) * Number of Meal Plans Created per User per Week * Number of Grocery Lists Generated per User per Week * User Retention Rate (Day 7, 30, 90) * Average Rating of Recommended Meals (in-app feedback) * Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) * Net Promoter Score (NPS) * **Tracking Tools:** Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, Crashlytics, etc. ### **10. Future Considerations / Roadmap (Post-MVP)** * Direct integration with online grocery delivery services. * Advanced nutrition tracking (macro/micronutrients, calorie tracking). * Barcode scanning for inventory and adding items to the grocery list. * Multi-profile support for families. * Integration with smart kitchen appliances (e.g., smart fridges, ovens). * Community features (share recipes, meal plans, tips). * Premium subscription model (ad-free, advanced features, exclusive recipes). --- ### **Next Steps:** This PRD serves as a foundational document.
